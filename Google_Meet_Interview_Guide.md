## 4Ô∏è‚É£ How Do You Handle Objections from Customers?

### Your Answer (60-75 seconds)

*"Objection handling is one of my core strengths, and I see objections as opportunities to provide clarity rather than roadblocks.*

*My approach follows four steps:*

**1. Listen Fully**
*I never interrupt. I let the client express their concern completely. Often, objections mask deeper issues, so I listen to understand the root cause.*

**2. Acknowledge and Validate**
*I validate their concern. For example, if they say, 'It's too expensive,' I respond with, 'I completely understand‚Äî‚Çπ70,000 is a significant investment, and you want to ensure it's worth it.' This builds empathy and trust.*

**3. Reframe the Objection**
*I reframe their concern into a benefit. For pricing objections, I break down ROI: 'This certification could position you for board roles paying ‚Çπ5-10 lakhs annually. Compared to that potential, this is a strategic career investment.' I shift the conversation from cost to value.*

**4. Provide Evidence**
*I use social proof‚Äîsuccess stories, testimonials, case studies. For example, 'We had a client similar to your profile who was hesitant, but after completing the program, he secured two board positions within six months.'*

**Common Objections I Handle:**

**'I don't have time':**
*'I hear you‚Äîyou're already managing a demanding role. The program is designed for working professionals like you, with flexible, self-paced modules. Most of our successful students dedicate just 5-7 hours a week. Can we explore a timeline that works for you?'*

**'I'll think about it':**
*'Absolutely, this is an important decision. To help you think through it, can I share what specific concerns you'd like to evaluate? That way, I can provide the right information to make your decision easier.'*

**'I need to discuss with my family':**
*'That makes perfect sense‚Äîfamily support is important. Would it help if I provided a detailed brochure or even set up a brief call with your spouse so they understand the value and career impact?'*

*The key is to stay calm, never get defensive, and always guide the conversation back to their goals and the solution we're offering."*

**Key Techniques:**

- Listen first, respond second
- Empathy and validation
- Reframing objections as opportunities
- Evidence-based responses
- Never pushy, always consultative

## üéØ Google Meet Interview Success Tips

### Before the Interview ‚úÖ

- Test your internet, camera, and mic 30 minutes before
- Dress professionally (at least business casual from waist up)
- Choose a clean, well-lit background
- Keep your resume, notes, and a notebook handy
- Review your key stories and metrics
- Be in a quiet, distraction-free space

### During the Interview ‚úÖ

- Join 2-3 minutes early
- Maintain eye contact (look at the camera, not the screen)
- Smile and show positive body language
- Speak clearly and at a moderate pace
- Use the STAR method for behavioral questions
- Take brief pauses to think before answering tough questions
- Show enthusiasm and energy
- Use specific examples and numbers

### Body Language ‚úÖ

- Sit upright with good posture
- Use hand gestures naturally (but not excessively)
- Nod to show active listening
- Avoid fidgeting or looking away frequently
- Mirror the interviewer's energy level

### Don'ts ‚ùå

- Don't read from a script (use notes sparingly)
- Don't have distracting tabs or notifications open
- Don't interrupt the interviewer
- Don't speak negatively about past employers
- Don't rush through answers
- Don't forget to ask your own questions at the end
